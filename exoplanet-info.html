Exoplanet Information

Details about specific exoplanets.

51 Pegasi b

51 Pegasi b, discovered in 1995, is the first confirmed exoplanet orbiting a Sun-like star. It has a mass similar to that of Jupiter and orbits its star every four days.

Orbit Details: Completes its orbit around its host star in just 4.2 days, making it a "Hot Jupiter." Despite its size, the planet‚Äôs proximity leads to extreme temperatures.

Distance from Earth: About 50 light-years away in the Pegasus constellation.

Significance: This discovery marked a new era in the study of exoplanets.

TRAPPIST-1 System

The TRAPPIST-1 system is a treasure trove of seven rocky planets, some of which reside in the habitable zone, making them prime candidates for the search for extraterrestrial life.

Number of Planets: Seven planets, three of which are located in the habitable zone.

Star Type: TRAPPIST-1 is an ultra-cool dwarf star, much smaller and cooler than the Sun.

Future Exploration: These planets are prime candidates for space missions studying atmospheres and potential life.

Kepler-452b

Kepler-452b, often called 'Earth‚Äôs cousin,' was discovered in 2015. This exoplanet is in the habitable zone of a star similar to our Sun, suggesting it might have Earth-like conditions.

Orbit Period: 385 days, longer than Earth‚Äôs orbit.

Distance from Earth: Located 1,400 light-years away in the Cygnus constellation.

Planetary Characteristics: Its size and location in the habitable zone make it a compelling target for life searches.

Proxima Centauri b

Proxima Centauri b is the closest known exoplanet to Earth, lying in the habitable zone of its star. Its discovery has sparked interest in the potential for life beyond our solar system.

Star Distance: Located just 4.24 light-years away from Earth, making it the closest known exoplanet.

Potential for Life: Its position in the habitable zone suggests liquid water may exist on its surface.

Mission Interest: A key target for interstellar missions like Breakthrough Starshot.

Gliese 581g

Gliese 581g orbits the red dwarf star Gliese 581 and is often discussed as a potentially habitable planet. Its location within the habitable zone allows for the possibility of liquid water.

Discovery Debates: Some astronomers debate whether it exists, while others see it as one of the most Earth-like exoplanets.

Star Characteristics: Gliese 581g orbits a cooler red dwarf star, but its close proximity makes it a candidate for life.

Possibility of Life: Its habitable zone location raises the possibility of liquid water on its surface.

HD 209458 b

HD 209458 b, also known as Osiris, was one of the first exoplanets found transiting its host star. It's a gas giant, and studies have revealed a faint atmosphere surrounding the planet.

Atmospheric Composition: Its escaping hydrogen atmosphere led to its nickname "Osiris."

Significance of Discovery: First exoplanet observed transiting its star, allowing direct measurement of its size and atmosphere.

Atmospheric Loss: The planet is losing its atmosphere due to the heat from its star, forming a large gas cloud.
